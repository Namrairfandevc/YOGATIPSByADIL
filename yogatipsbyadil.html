Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

The foundation of all standing poses, Mountain Pose improves posture and balance while grounding your energy.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet together, heels slightly apart
  2. Distribute weight evenly across both feet
  3. Engage your thighs and lift your kneecaps
  4. Lengthen your tailbone toward the floor
  5. Roll shoulders back and down
  6. Extend arms alongside body, palms facing forward
  7. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute, breathing deeply
Benefits: Improves posture, strengthens thighs and ankles, increases awareness

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

One of the most recognized yoga poses, Downward Dog energizes and rejuvenates the entire body.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Start on hands and knees in tabletop position
  2. Spread fingers wide, press palms firmly
  3. Tuck toes and lift hips up and back
  4. Straighten legs without locking knees
  5. Press heels toward floor (they don't need to touch)
  6. Relax neck and let head hang
  7. Hold for 1-3 minutes, breathing steadily
Benefits: Stretches hamstrings and calves, strengthens arms and legs, relieves stress

Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

A balancing pose that strengthens legs and core while improving focus and concentration.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Begin in Mountain Pose
  2. Shift weight to left foot, ground firmly
  3. Bend right knee and place foot on inner left thigh
  4. Press foot and thigh together
  5. Bring hands to prayer position at chest
  6. Optional: raise arms overhead
  7. Hold for 30 seconds, switch sides
Benefits: Improves balance, strengthens ankles and legs, enhances concentration

Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

A powerful standing pose that builds strength, stamina, and confidence.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Step right foot forward about 3-4 feet
  2. Turn left foot out 45 degrees
  3. Bend right knee to 90 degrees
  4. Square hips to front of mat
  5. Raise arms overhead, palms facing each other
  6. Look up at thumbs, lift chest
  7. Hold 30 seconds to 1 minute, switch sides
Benefits: Strengthens legs and core, stretches chest and shoulders, improves focus

Child's Pose (Balasana)

A gentle resting pose that calms the mind and relieves stress and tension.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Kneel on mat with knees hip-width apart
  2. Sit back on heels
  3. Fold forward, extending arms in front
  4. Rest forehead on mat
  5. Relax shoulders and breathe deeply
  6. Hold for 1-5 minutes
  7. Use as rest pose whenever needed
Benefits: Calms nervous system, stretches hips and thighs, relieves back pain

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

A backbend that opens the chest and strengthens the spine while energizing the body.

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Lie face down with legs extended
  2. Place palms under shoulders
  3. Press tops of feet into mat
  4. Inhale and straighten arms, lifting chest
  5. Keep elbows slightly bent
  6. Draw shoulders back and down
  7. Hold for 15-30 seconds, release slowly
Benefits: Strengthens spine, stretches chest and lungs, stimulates abdominal organs

Essential Yoga Tips

Practice Timing

Practice yoga early morning on an empty stomach for maximum benefits. If morning isn't possible, wait 3-4 hours after meals.

üßò

Consistency Over Intensity

Regular practice is more important than long sessions. Even 15-20 minutes daily is better than occasional long practices.

üí®

Focus on Breathing

Breath is the bridge between body and mind. Coordinate movements with breath and practice pranayama regularly.

üéØ

Listen to Your Body

Never force yourself into a pose. Respect your body's limits and progress gradually to avoid injury.

üè†

Create Sacred Space

Dedicate a quiet, clean space for practice. Use a quality yoga mat and keep the area free from distractions.

üíß

Stay Hydrated

Drink water before practice but not during. Hydrate well after your session to help with recovery.

üëï

Wear Comfortable Clothes

Choose breathable, stretchy clothing that allows full range of motion without being too loose.

üéº

End with Savasana

Always end your practice with final relaxation pose. This allows your body to integrate the benefits.

üìñ

Study Yoga Philosophy

Yoga is more than physical practice. Learn about its philosophy through books and teachings.
